About Me

// my story

I am Mohammed Emad AL-Mekhlafi, a Cybersecurity and Web Developer Engineer from Sana'a, Yemen. My passion for technology was born in an environment of extreme adversity. Growing up amidst conflict and severe economic hardship, I found my refuge and purpose in the digital world.

Currently, a first-year Cybersecurity student at Al-Razi University, I quickly realized that the local educational infrastructure and outdated curricula could not keep pace with global technological advancements. Faced with a severe lack of modern resources, I took my education into my own hands. I turned to the internet, teaching myself advanced programming, systems administration, and network security, proving that self-driven learning can transcend physical borders.

However, self-learning in a constrained environment has its limits. I am actively seeking international scholarships to continue my education in a world-class institution. My vision extends beyond personal success; I am a fierce advocate for the Free and Open Source Software (FOSS) movement. I believe that high-quality education and technical knowledge should be freely accessible to everyone, provided it is used ethically to build communities, not harm them.

I am driven, innovative, and ready to leave a historical mark in the cybersecurity field. All I need is the opportunity.
